About Penang

Penang is a Malaysian state located on the northwest coast of Peninsular Malaysia, by the Malacca Strait. Capital city in Penang is Georgetown.

Penang’s population stand at nearly 1.75 million as of 2017. Penang’s modern history began in 1786, when Francis Light created Georgetown as a trading post of the British East India Company. Penang had been formed as a part of the Straits Settlements in 1826, which became a British crown colony in 1867. Direct British rule was only stop during World War II. British back to Penang at 1945. After that, Penang was combined with the Federation of Malaya, which gained independence from the British Empire in 1957. The decline of its entrepot trade towards in 1970, Penang’s economy was reoriented to hi-tech manufacturing.
